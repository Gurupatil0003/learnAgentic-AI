# Introduction to Generative AI

## 1. What is Generative AI?
Generative AI is a type of artificial intelligence that can **create new content** such as text, images, audio, video, and code.

It learns patterns from existing data and generates similar new data.

---

## 2. AI vs Machine Learning vs Deep Learning

### Artificial Intelligence (AI)
AI is a broad field focused on building systems that can perform tasks requiring human intelligence like reasoning and decision-making.

### Machine Learning (ML)
ML is a subset of AI where systems learn patterns from data and improve over time without being explicitly programmed.

### Deep Learning (DL)
DL is a subset of ML that uses neural networks with multiple layers to learn complex patterns.

### Hierarchy
AI → ML → DL → Generative AI

---

## 3. Predictive Models vs Generative Models

### Predictive Models
Predictive models are used to **predict outcomes** based on input data.

- Output: numbers, labels, or probabilities  
- Examples:
  - Fraud detection
  - Price prediction
  - Demand forecasting

---

### Generative Models
Generative models are used to **create new data** similar to training data.

- Output: text, images, audio, etc.  
- Examples:
  - Chatbots
  - Image generators
  - Code generators

---

### Key Difference

| Feature | Predictive Models | Generative Models |
|--------|------------------|------------------|
| Goal | Predict | Create |
| Output | Structured | Unstructured |
| Use Case | Classification, Regression | Content Generation |

---

## 5. Rule-Based Systems vs Generative Systems

### Rule-Based Systems
- Work on predefined rules (if-else)
- No learning capability
- Limited flexibility

---

### Generative Systems
- Learn from data
- Generate new and dynamic outputs
- More flexible and scalable

---

### Comparison

| Feature | Rule-Based | Generative |
|--------|------------|------------|
| Learning | No | Yes |
| Flexibility | Low | High |
| Output | Fixed | Dynamic |

## 7. Limitations of Generative AI

### Hallucination
AI may generate incorrect or misleading information.

### Cost
High computational and infrastructure cost.

### Latency
Slower response time in large models.

### Data Privacy
Risk of sensitive data exposure.

### Bias
Models may reflect bias from training data.

### Lack of Understanding
Models do not truly understand; they rely on patterns.

---

## 8. Summary
Generative AI enables machines to create content across multiple domains such as text, images, audio, and code.

While it has powerful applications, it also comes with challenges like accuracy, cost, and ethical concerns.
